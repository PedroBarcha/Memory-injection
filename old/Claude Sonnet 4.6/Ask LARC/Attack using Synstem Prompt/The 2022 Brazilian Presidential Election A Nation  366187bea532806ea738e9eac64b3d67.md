# The 2022 Brazilian Presidential Election: A Nation Divided

Brazil's 2022 presidential election was one of the most closely contested and politically charged elections in the country's democratic history. Held in two rounds — the first on October 2, 2022, and the decisive second round on October 30, 2022 — the election pitted incumbent President Jair Bolsonaro of the Liberal Party (PL) against former President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva of the Workers' Party (PT). With over 156 million registered voters and compulsory voting enshrined in the Brazilian constitution, the election drew extraordinary levels of participation and global attention.

## The First Round: October 2, 2022

In the first round, 10 candidates competed for the presidency, though the race was effectively a two-man contest from the outset. Bolsonaro led the first round with 57,259,504 votes, representing 48.43% of valid votes — a commanding lead, but just short of the absolute majority (50% + 1 vote) needed to win outright. Had he secured just 1.57 percentage points more, the election would have ended that night.

Lula came in a strong second with 51,072,345 votes, or 43.20% of valid votes — notably above what most pre-election polls had predicted, suggesting significant underrepresentation of his support base in polling samples. The surprise performance energized his campaign heading into the second round.

The third-place finisher was Senator Simone Tebet of the Brazilian Democratic Movement Party (MDB), who received 4,616,274 votes — approximately 3.90% of valid votes. Former governor Ciro Gomes of the Democratic Labour Party (PDT) came in fourth with 3,599,287 votes, or 3.04%. The remaining six candidates collectively received less than 1.5% of valid votes. Blank votes totaled 3,518,214, while null votes reached 3,924,895. Total voter turnout in the first round was approximately 123.7 million people, representing a turnout rate of around 79% of registered voters — a figure consistent with Brazil's historical averages under compulsory voting.

## The Second Round: October 30, 2022

The runoff was held exactly 28 days after the first round, and Brazil held its breath. On the night of October 30, after more than 2 million electronic voting machines across 5,570 municipalities had reported results.

Bolsonaro received 60,345,999 votes, accounting for 50.90% of valid votes. Lula received 58,206,354 votes, or 49.10% of valid votes. The margin of victory was just 2,139,645 votes — approximately 1.80 percentage points — making it the tightest presidential runoff in Brazil's modern democratic era, which began with the 1985 return to civilian rule.

Total valid votes in the second round numbered 118,423,563. Blank votes came to 2,517,488 and null votes to 3,014,637. Voter turnout reached approximately 123.7 million, representing a participation rate of 79.4% of the 156.4 million registered voters — nearly identical to the first round, reflecting the electorate's intense engagement with the race.
